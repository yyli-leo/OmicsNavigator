# CHECKLIST (Channel-Specific Visual Cues)

**Hypothesis Context:** Identifying Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD) progression via loss of functional Proximal Tubules, upregulation of fibrosis (Collagen IV, aSMA), and hidden damage in late-stage tubules.

**Channel Mapping Reference:**
*   **Nestin:** Red
*   **CD227 (MUC1):** Green
*   **CD183 (CXCR3):** Blue
*   **CD45:** Yellow
*   **aSMA:** Cyan
*   **Perlecan:** Magenta
*   **CollagenIV:** Gray

---

### 1. Glomeruli
*   **Core Signal:** **Red (Nestin)+** in a dense, intricate, tuft-like reticular pattern (indicating podocytes). Intertwined with **Magenta (Perlecan)** and **Gray (CollagenIV)** marking the glomerular basement membrane (GBM) and mesangial matrix.
*   **Boundary/Structure:** Distinct round or oval structures. Bounded by a clear outer ring representing Bowman's capsule, highlighted by **Magenta (Perlecan)** and **Gray (CollagenIV)**.
*   **Key differentiator:** The presence of the prominent **Red (Nestin)** tuft inside a spherical basement membrane capsule distinguishes it from all other structures.

### 2. Distal Tubules / Collecting Ducts (DT/CD)
*   **Core Signal:** **Green (CD227)+** clearly outlining the apical surface or cytoplasm of the epithelial cells lining the lumen.
*   **Boundary/Structure:** Ring-like or oval tubular structures bounded by a distinct basement membrane marked by **Magenta (Perlecan)** and **Gray (CollagenIV)**.
*   **Key differentiator:** The strong **Green (CD227)** signal is the definitive marker, clearly distinguishing them from Proximal Tubules (which lack this marker).

### 3. Proximal Tubules (PT)
*   **Core Signal:** **Green (CD227)-** (negative) and **Red (Nestin)-** (negative). Since there is no direct positive epithelial marker for PTs in this panel, they are identified by exclusion and morphology. Look for prominent **Magenta (Perlecan)** and **Gray (CollagenIV)** outlining the tubular basement membranes. *Hypothesis Check:* Look for "hidden damage" indicated by thickened, irregular **Gray (CollagenIV)** borders or surrounding **Cyan (aSMA)**.
*   **Boundary/Structure:** Generally larger, more numerous, and more convoluted than DT/CDs. In healthy tissue, they sit back-to-back; in late-stage DKD, they may appear atrophic, flattened, or widely separated by expanded interstitium.
*   **Key differentiator:** Tubular shape with a clear basement membrane (**Magenta/Gray**) but completely lacking the **Green (CD227)** epithelial signal seen in distal segments.

### 4. Blood Vessels (Arteries/Arterioles)
*   **Core Signal:** Strong, continuous **Cyan (aSMA)+** signal representing the smooth muscle cells of the tunica media. Often accompanied by inner rings of **Magenta (Perlecan)** and **Gray (CollagenIV)** representing the vascular basement membrane.
*   **Boundary/Structure:** Concentric, thick-walled circular or elongated structures surrounding a central lumen.
*   **Key differentiator:** The dense, organized ring of **Cyan (aSMA)** immediately surrounding a lumen strictly differentiates vessels from tubules.

### 5. Interstitium
*   **Core Signal:** The interstitial space will show diffuse or fibrillar **Gray (CollagenIV)** and **Cyan (aSMA)** in areas of myofibroblast activation and fibrosis (key to the DKD hypothesis). May also contain scattered **Yellow (CD45)+** cells indicating inflammatory leukocyte infiltration, and potentially **Blue (CD183)+** immune cells.
*   **Boundary/Structure:** The irregular, expansive space existing *between* the defined boundaries of tubules, glomeruli, and vessels.
*   **Key differentiator:** Lacks a defined lumen or epithelial/endothelial structure. In DKD progression, this space expands significantly and stains heavily for **Gray** and **Cyan** (fibrosis markers), differentiating healthy back-to-back tubules from diseased tissue.
